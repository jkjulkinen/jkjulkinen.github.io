Developing Data Products Project

Application to compare Expected MPG vs real MPG

Jari Kaljunen

MPG as criteria

Miles Per Gallon (MPG) is important factor when comparing different car models.

However, if you are not interested in small cars, why should you bother to having them at your comparison list?

If you want automatic transmission for your comfort, why should you see MPG for manual cars?

Finally, if the MPG actually goes low when the car gets heavier, which car makes the best of that?

Now you can compare cars by the result versus general expectation, which car makes best towards expectations!

Use the app on https://jkjulkinen.shinyapps.io/DevelopingDataProductsProj/ and see the facts.

The source data

The data source:

  1. was extracted from the 1974 Motor Trend US magazine
  2. comprises fuel consumption
  3. 10 aspects of automobile design and performance
  4. 32 automobiles (1973-74 models)
  5. Achieved from R Datasets

The logics

The basic rule is that when the car gets heavier, the MPG gets lower.

That is a linear model that can be definied with R Language with:

lm(mpg ~ wt, data=mtcars)

## 
## Call:
## lm(formula = mpg ~ wt, data = mtcars)
## 
## Coefficients:
## (Intercept)           wt  
##      37.285       -5.344

That says us that when weigth gets up with 1000 lb, MPG gets down with -5.3444716.

What we do

The basic rule is that when the car gets heavier, the MPG gets lower.

But you are basically interested in cars in some particular weight interval. We let you choose the weight interval of your interest and calculate the expectations for that interval.

You can choose if you want to see automatic or manual cars listed.

That car which makes best towards the expectation is shown first at the result list!!
